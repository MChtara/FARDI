PHASE 5: Execution & Problem-Solving
Sub-phase 5.1: Handling a Last-Minute Issue  
• Description: In this phase, undergraduate students develop English language skills by simulating the management of a last-minute problem during the "Global Cultures Festival" (e.g., a performer cancels, bad weather, technical issue with sound system). The focus is on writing clear emails, messages, or announcements to solve the problem, negotiating solutions, and maintaining polite and professional communication. The phase follows the 5E pedagogical model (Engage, Explore, Explain, Elaborate, Evaluate) to build problem-solving vocabulary, conditional structures, polite requests, suggestions, and formal/informal register, aligned with CEFR levels A1 to C1. Emphasis is on autonomous writing with error detection.
• Scoring Main: A1=1pt, A2=2pts, B1=3pts, B2=4pts, C1=5pts. Total ≥ 20 to proceed.
• Scoring Remedial: 8/10 correct to proceed.
STEP 1: Engage
• Description: Spark interest by connecting to prior knowledge of unexpected problems during events; students discuss real-life situations where something went wrong and how it was fixed. To engage, Ms. Mabrouki shares two real examples:

A festival cancellation email example: https://theeventscalendar.com/blog/event-cancellation-announcement-examples/
A last-minute change announcement on social media: https://twitter.com/example/status/change-post
Students then play "Wordshake" individually (link: https://learnenglishteens.britishcouncil.org/vocabulary/vocabulary-games/wordshake) for 3 minutes to form problem-solving words (target: problem, cancel, change, solution, sorry, alternative, fix, urgent), preparing for discussion.
• Learning Outcomes (A1/A2/B1/B2): A1: Can name basic problem words (e.g., "problem", "cancel"). A2: Can describe a simple problem (e.g., "Performer cancel because sick"). B1: Can explain why a solution works (e.g., "Change performer because alternative"). B2: Can compare solutions (e.g., "Email better than announcement for formal"). C1: Can critique communication strategies (e.g., "Apology and alternative maintain trust").

MAIN ACTIVITY (SCENARIO)
• Scenario Intro: The committee is preparing the Global Cultures Festival when a last-minute issue happens: the main singer cancels due to illness. Ms. Mabrouki says: "We have a big problem! The singer just canceled. Let's discuss how to handle this quickly and professionally."

INTERACTION 2
• Instructor: SKANDER
• Dialogue: "The singer canceled! How can we solve this last-minute problem for the festival?"
• Instruction: Suggest one solution (e.g., find alternative performer, change schedule) and why it works, incorporating glossary terms (e.g., "alternative", "urgent", "solution").
• Hint: Start with "We can..." or "I suggest..."
• Assessment Focus: Grammar (connectors like "because"), Problem-solving language.
• Evaluation Note: Evaluate for logical reasoning; must include solution and at least one vocabulary term.
• Expected Response A1: Find new singer.
• Expected Response A2: Find alternative singer because urgent.
• Expected Response B1: We can find another singer as an alternative because it is urgent and keeps the program.
• Expected Response B2: I suggest finding a backup performer or local talent as a quick alternative, since this solution is urgent and maintains the event quality.
• Expected Response C1: A feasible solution would be to secure a substitute artist immediately while sending an apologetic update to attendees, ensuring minimal disruption and preserving trust.

STEP 2: Explore
• Description: Students explore real crisis communication examples (e.g., event organizer tweets during a power outage) and identify key elements: calm tone, quick facts, solution, next steps. They write short trial messages (e.g., 3-sentence announcement) using a simple template. Gamified with "Sushi Spell" (link: https://learnenglishteens.britishcouncil.org/vocabulary/vocabulary-games/sushi-spell) - spell 5 terms (emergency, backup, announce, update, communicate) before writing.
• Learning Outcomes: A2: Write 2-3 sentence announcements. B1: Write 4-6 sentence updates with reasons. B2: Write structured emails/announcements with polite language. C1: Write multi-channel crisis responses with strategic tone.
MAIN ACTIVITY (SCENARIO)
• Scenario Intro: "One hour before the Global Cultures Festival opens, the main stage lights fail! We need to write quick solutions: announcement to audience, email to sponsors, social media update. Let's explore real examples first."
INTERACTION 1
• Instructor: Ms. Mabrouki
• Dialogue: "Play Sushi Spell to activate vocabulary, then write a short announcement."
• Instruction: Play game, then write 3-6 sentences announcing the issue and solution.
• Expected Response A2: Lights problem. Use backup. Come festival.
• Expected Response B1: Dear guests, there is a lighting problem on stage. We are using backup lights now. The festival will start on time. Thank you for understanding.
• Expected Response B2: Urgent update: Due to a technical issue, the main stage lighting has temporarily failed. Our team is implementing the backup lighting system and expects resolution within 20 minutes. The event will proceed as scheduled. We appreciate your patience and understanding.
• Expected Response C1: Immediate notice to all attendees: An unexpected technical failure has affected the main stage lighting system just one hour before opening. Our contingency team is actively deploying the pre-tested backup lighting array, with full restoration anticipated within the next 20-25 minutes. The festival schedule remains unchanged, and we are committed to delivering the full cultural experience you expect. We sincerely thank you for your patience and understanding during this brief disruption.

• Description: Assess learning by correcting provided "wrong" versions of crisis communication texts written in Step 4 (social media announcements, emails to sponsors/team, follow-up updates, contingency plans). Spelling
Grammar
Coherence/cohesion, tone & politeness
Vocabulary, structure & overall crisis communication quality

This develops autonomous editing and self-assessment skills for real-world professional crisis writing.
To gamify, use "Wordshake" (link: https://learnenglishteens.britishcouncil.org/vocabulary/vocabulary-games/wordshake) after correction: Form feedback terms like "strength", "weakness", "calm", "transparent", "reassure", "resolve" to "refine" scores.
• Learning Outcomes (A2/B1/B2/C1):
A2: Can spot and fix basic spelling/grammar in short crisis messages.
B1: Can correct grammar, simple structure, polite tone, and basic vocabulary.
B2: Can improve coherence, cohesion, calm tone, vocabulary precision, and logical crisis flow.
C1: Can deliver nuanced, professional-level corrections across all dimensions (tone, strategy, stakeholder sensitivity, persuasive reassurance), achieving full autonomy in crisis communication writing.

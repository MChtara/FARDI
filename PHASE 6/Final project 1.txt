Final project 
1. Written Portfolio (50% of final project score) 
Submit 3 short documents (total ~400-800 words depending on your level):
o Personal Reflection Report (300-500 words) Answer these questions in your own words (use past tense, be honest):
1. What was your favorite moment of the festival and why?
2. What was the most difficult moment and how did you/your team solve it?
3. What new English words/grammar did you learn during the program?
4. How did your English improve from the beginning to now? Give one example.
5. What would you do differently if we organize another festival?
o Revised Post-Event Report Summary (150-300 words) Take your earlier report summary (from Phase 6.1) and improve it after peer feedback. Add at least one specific number or example (e.g., "over 800 people came", "guests said the dances were amazing").
o One Piece of Feedback You Received + Your Response (100-200 words) Copy one piece of feedback from a classmate (or teacher). Write:
1. What did they say?
2. Do you agree? Why / why not?
3. What will you change because of this feedback?
Important Rules - No AI Allowed for This Assignment
* Write everything yourself (no ChatGPT, Grok, Grammarly rewrite, etc.).
* We will check for AI patterns (sudden jump in level, unnatural phrasing, lack of personal voice).
* You must be able to explain every sentence during the oral defense.
* If AI use is detected, the written part = 0 points (but you can still do the oral part).

2. Students should write short scripts (3-8 minutes when performed) to be played live by groups of students or individually at the end of your program.

These themes are designed to:
* Fit the A2 ? B2 progression (simple language at lower levels, more complex structures/dialogue at higher levels)
* Directly connect to the content of your 6-phase training (festival planning, social media, crisis management, instructions, reflection, feedback)
* Encourage creativity, personalization, and use of the vocabulary/grammar learned
* Allow natural integration of speaking skills (dialogue, pronunciation, fluency, interaction)
* Be performable in small groups (3-6 students) with minimal props
Suggested Themes for Live Script Performances
1. "The Day Before the Festival - Chaos & Teamwork"
o Setting: Committee meeting the night before opening
o Plot: One problem after another (lights not working, volunteer late, social media post mistake, food shortage). Students must solve it together using instructions, polite requests, contingency plans, and encouragement.
o Key language: imperatives, please/thank you, first/then/next, emergency/backup, reassure, team communication
o Why it works: Revisits Phase 5 (crisis & instructions), allows humor, shows collaboration.
2. "Volunteer Training Day - The Funny Mistakes"
o Setting: Ms. Mabrouki (or a student playing her) training new volunteers
o Plot: Volunteers misunderstand instructions (e.g., guide guests to wrong booth, panic over small queue, forget to say welcome). Trainer corrects with clear, polite instructions. Ends with successful rehearsal.
o Key language: imperatives, sequencing words, polite requests, careful/safety, repeat/check/confirm
o Why it works: Directly tests Sub-phase 5.2 content, funny and relatable, great for practicing clear speech.
3. "The Viral Post Disaster"
o Setting: Social media team reacting to a bad post (wrong date, offensive emoji, no CTA) that goes viral for the wrong reasons
o Plot: Panic, crisis communication, write a quick apology/update, turn it into a positive story.
o Key language: call-to-action, hashtag, emoji, transparent, announce/update, reassure
o Why it works: Revisits Phase 4, modern and funny, shows real-world social media consequences.
4. "The Post-Festival Debrief - Honest Feedback Time"
o Setting: Committee + volunteers meeting after the event
o Plot: Share successes ("The dances were amazing!"), challenges ("Lighting almost ruined it"), give constructive feedback to each other, make recommendations for next year.
o Key language: strength/weakness, feedback, positive/constructive, suggestion, recommend, improve
o Why it works: Ties directly to Phase 6, practices Sub-phase 6.2 peer feedback language, reflective and emotional.
5. "A Day in the Life of a Festival Volunteer"
o Setting: Follow one volunteer from morning briefing to end of day
o Plot: Morning instructions from coordinator ? welcoming guests ? managing queue ? handling small crisis (lost child, spilled food) ? debrief with team.
o Key language: imperatives, please/thank you, first/then/next, careful/safety, help, guide, welcome
o Why it works: Combines Sub-phase 5.2 instructions + crisis management, narrative structure, good for acting.
6. "The Sponsor Call - Crisis Communication in Action"
o Setting: Phone/video call between festival organizer and angry sponsor after lighting failure
o Plot: Sponsor upset ? organizer uses transparent, reassuring, professional language ? explains solution ? ends positively with thanks and future promise.
o Key language: transparent, reassure, immediate response, resolve, thank you for understanding, appreciate your support
o Why it works: Revisits Phase 5 crisis communication, practices formal/polite spoken English.
7. "Time Travel Festival Meeting" (creative/fun option)
o Setting: Committee from "last year" meets committee from "this year"
o Plot: Last year's team shares mistakes ? this year's team explains how they improved (better planning, backups, instructions) ? both agree on recommendations for next year.
o Key language: past tense narration, improve, lesson learned, recommend, success/challenge
o Why it works: Allows reflection on the whole program, funny time-travel element, good for storytelling.
Practical Implementation Tips
* Length: 5-8 minutes 
* Group size: 3-6 students per group or individual
* Preparation time: 2-4 hours in-class + homework (write script ? rehearse ? refine after peer feedback)
* Performance: Live in final class(es) 
* Evaluation criteria (simple rubric):
o Content relevance to theme (20%)
o Use of target vocabulary/grammar (25%)
o Fluency & pronunciation (20%)
o Interaction & teamwork (15%)
o Creativity & engagement (10%)
o Clarity & politeness (10%)
My Recommendation
The theme is assigned randomly. This gives variety in the final performances and let teachers see how well students apply different parts of the program (crisis language, instructions, reflection, feedback).

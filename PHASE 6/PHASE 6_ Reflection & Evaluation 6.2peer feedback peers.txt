PHASE 6: Reflection & Evaluation
Sub-phase 6.2: Peer Feedback Discussion
PHASE OVERVIEW
• Phase Title: Phase 6: Reflection & Evaluation - 6.2 Peer Feedback Discussion
• Description: In this sub-phase, students engage in structured peer feedback discussions on each other's post-event reports . They practice giving and receiving constructive, specific, polite feedback using sentence starters, positive sandwich technique (positive ? suggestion ? positive), and reflective listening. Focus is on oral and written feedback skills, empathy, active listening, and collaborative improvement. The sub-phase follows the 5E model to develop metacognitive and interpersonal language skills.
• Scoring Main: A2=1pt, B1=2pts, B2=3pts, C1=4pts. Total = 12 to proceed.
• Scoring Remedial: 6/8 correct to pass.
STEP 1: Engage
• Description: Re-engage students emotionally with the value of feedback by showing a short, relatable video clip of people giving and receiving constructive feedback a TED-Ed on "How to Give Good Feedback" LINK https://youtu.be/wtl5UrrgU8c?si=p-QUPpuQl2XqY5iw 
Ms. Mabrouki asks: "Have you ever received feedback that helped you improve? How did it feel? What makes feedback good or bad?" To activate vocabulary, students play "Wordshake" individually for 3 minutes to form words related to peer feedback (target: feedback, constructive, positive, suggestion, strength, weakness, improve, polite, listen, agree, disagree, helpful). This prepares them to share personal experiences and understand the purpose of peer discussion.
• Learning Outcomes (A2/B1/B2/C1):
A2: Can name basic feedback words (e.g., "good", "help", "listen").
B1: Can describe simple experiences with feedback (e.g., "Feedback helped me").
B2: Can explain why constructive feedback is valuable (e.g., "It shows what is good and what to improve").
C1: Can critically reflect on the role of feedback in personal and team growth (e.g., "Effective peer feedback fosters self-awareness and collaborative improvement").

STEP 2: Explore
(Sub-phase 6.2: Peer Feedback Discussion - Phase 6: Reflection & Evaluation)
• Description: Students actively explore the purpose, types, and real-world techniques of giving and receiving constructive peer feedback by reviewing short video clips, sample peer comments, and feedback sentence starters. They experiment with writing and speaking short trial feedback messages (positive comments, specific suggestions, polite phrasing) using guided templates for different report sections (summary, successes, challenges, recommendations). The goal is discovery: trying out what makes feedback helpful, respectful, motivating, and actionable before formal explanation in Step 3.
To gamify, use "Sushi Spell" (link: https://learnenglishteens.britishcouncil.org/vocabulary/vocabulary-games/sushi-spell) - spell 5 feedback words (feedback, constructive, positive, suggestion, improve) before writing the first trial feedback.
• Learning Outcomes (A2/B1/B2/C1):
A2: Can write 2-4 very simple positive or helpful comments following examples.
B1: Can write short feedback messages with polite language and basic suggestions.
B2: Can create structured, balanced feedback (positive + suggestion + positive) with specific examples.
C1: Can experiment with nuanced, empathetic, growth-oriented feedback that addresses tone, evidence, structure, and strategic recommendations.

STEP 3: Explain
(Sub-phase 6.2: Peer Feedback Discussion - Phase 6: Reflection & Evaluation)
• Description: Formalize concepts by defining and explaining the key principles and best practices of giving and receiving constructive peer feedback, especially in an academic/project context like reviewing post-event reports. Students learn the purpose, structure, language, and mindset of effective peer feedback through short explainer videos, real examples, and structured explanation tasks.
Incorporate glossary terms: "feedback", "constructive", "positive", "suggestion", "strength", "weakness", "improve", "specific", "actionable", "polite", "balanced", "sandwich technique", "empathy", "growth mindset", "listen", "agree", "disagree", "helpful".
Students watch a short explainer video on "How to Give and Receive Constructive Feedback" (or similar), complete listening tasks, then solidify vocabulary and concepts through flashcard matching and spelling games.
To gamify, begin with "Wordshake" (link: https://learnenglishteens.britishcouncil.org/vocabulary/vocabulary-games/wordshake) - 3 minutes to form words like "feedback", "constructive", "positive", "suggestion", "specific", "polite", "empathy", then explain one term.
• Learning Outcomes (A2/B1/B2/C1):
A2: Can define basic feedback words (e.g., "Positive is good things").
B1: Can explain simple functions with examples (e.g., "Suggestion is idea to make better because help").
B2: Can describe why constructive feedback should be specific, balanced, and polite (e.g., "Sandwich technique makes feedback easier to accept").
C1: Can analyze the role of empathy, growth mindset, and actionable language in effective peer feedback (e.g., "Empathetic tone reduces defensiveness and turns critique into collaborative growth").

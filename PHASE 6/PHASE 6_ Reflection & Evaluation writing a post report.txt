PHASE 6: Reflection & Evaluation
Sub-phase 6.1: Writing a Post-Event Report
PHASE OVERVIEW
• Phase Title: Phase 6: Reflection & Evaluation - 6.1 Writing a Post-Event Report
• Description: In this sub-phase, students write a structured post-event report for the Global Cultures Festival, summarizing successes, challenges, attendance, feedback, lessons learned, and recommendations. Focus is on past tense narration, objective reporting, connectors (however, therefore, in addition), formal tone, and balanced evaluation (strengths + weaknesses). The sub-phase follows the 5E model to consolidate reflective writing skills and metacognitive awareness.
• Scoring Main: A2=1pt, B1=2pts, B2=3pts, C1=4pts. Total = 12 to proceed.
• Scoring Remedial: 6/8 correct to pass.
STEP 1: Engage
• Description: Reconnect students emotionally and cognitively with the festival experience to motivate reflection. Ms. Mabrouki speaks about the Global Cultures Festival 
She asks: "What moments made you proud? What was difficult? How did it feel overall?" To activate vocabulary, students play "Wordshake" individually for 3 minutes to form reflection/reporting words (target: success, challenge, feedback, improve, achievement, strength, weakness, recommend, summary, positive, negative). This prepares them to share memories and start thinking about what goes into a post-event report.
• Learning Outcomes (A2/B1/B2/C1):
A2: Can name simple feelings and events (e.g., "happy", "good food").
B1: Can describe one success and one difficulty using past tense.
B2: Can explain why something was successful or challenging with reasons.
C1: Can critically reflect on personal contribution, overall impact, and value of evaluation.

STEP 3: Explain (Sub-phase 6.1: Writing a Post-Event Report - Phase 6: Reflection & Evaluation)

• Description: Formalize concepts by defining and explaining the key elements of a strong post-event report for the Global Cultures Festival. Students learn the standard structure, purpose, language features, and best practices of post-event reporting (summary, objective evaluation, balanced feedback, actionable recommendations). Incorporate glossary terms: "success", "challenge", "achievement", "strength", "weakness", "feedback", "improve", "recommend", "summary", "positive", "negative", "evidence", "impact", "lesson learned", "future improvement". Students watch a short explainer video on writing effective post-event reports, complete listening tasks, then practice definitions and explanations through flashcard matching and spelling games.

To gamify, start with "Wordshake" (link: https://learnenglishteens.britishcouncil.org/vocabulary/vocabulary-games/wordshake) - 3 minutes to form words like "success", "challenge", "feedback", "recommend", "summary", "improve", then explain one term.

• Learning Outcomes (A2/B1/B2/C1): A2: Can define basic report terms (e.g., "Success is good result"). B1: Can explain simple functions with examples (e.g., "Feedback is what people say because it helps us improve"). B2: Can describe why balance, evidence, and recommendations matter (e.g., "Include strengths and weaknesses because report must be honest"). C1: Can analyze the purpose and structure of a professional post-event report (e.g., "Balanced evaluation with evidence-based recommendations demonstrates transparency and drives continuous improvement").

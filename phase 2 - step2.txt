Phase 2 - Step 2: Scheduling Meetings
Learning Outcomes (For Implementers):
By the end of this step, students should demonstrate:
* A1 (Basic): Suggest simple meeting times and purposes with basic vocabulary, respond to suggestions with minimal reasoning, and describe basic plans.
* A2 (Elementary): Propose meeting schedules with basic connectors (e.g., "because"), respond to suggestions with simple reasoning, and describe plans with some detail.
* B1 (Intermediate): Suggest clear meeting schedules with moderate reasoning, respond politely with explanations, and describe plans with cultural connections.
* B2 (Upper-Intermediate): Propose detailed meeting schedules with strong reasoning, negotiate fluently with polite responses, and describe plans with cultural depth.
Implementer Notes: Use CEFR-aligned outcomes for feedback/scoring. Never display CEFR levels/scores to students. Use thematic feedback (e.g., "Great idea! Let's add more details!").
Scenario:
You're back in the lively committee room, filled with energy and colorful Tunisian decorations. The team is ready to schedule meetings to organize the cultural event's tasks (e.g., decorations, music, food, promotion). Your action plan needs a clear schedule to keep everyone on track. Work with Ms. Mabrouki and the team to propose meeting times, discuss their purpose, and ensure the plan aligns with the cultural theme from Phase 1.
Action Items:
1. Meeting Proposal Task
Instructions: Ms. Mabrouki says, "Let's schedule our first planning meeting! When should we meet, and why is that time perfect for our team?" Type or say a short proposal (2-3 sentences) suggesting a day, time, and reason tied to the team's needs or event goals.
Implementer Notes:
o Integration: Display Ms. Mabrouki's question with her avatar in the committee room (e.g., with Tunisian event posters). Provide a text box or voice input (if Grok's voice mode is enabled). Include a "Hint" button suggesting "Suggest a day and time, like 'Friday at 3 PM,' and a reason."
o Answers: Expect:
* A1: "Meet Friday. It good for team."
* A2: "I suggest Friday at 3 PM because team is free."
* B1: "Let's meet on Friday at 3 PM since most team members are available then."
* B2: "I propose Friday at 3 PM for our meeting, as it suits everyone's schedule and gives us time to plan the cultural event."
o Feedback: Use NLP to analyze vocabulary (e.g., "good" vs. "suits"), grammar (e.g., simple present vs. complex structures), and relevance to team needs. Deliver feedback via Ms. Mabrouki's avatar, e.g., "Nice suggestion, [Player]! Try 'Friday at 3 PM because everyone is free' for clarity." Never mention CEFR levels or technical terms.
o Scoring: A1 = 1, A2 = 2, B1 = 3, B2 = 4. B2 contributes to progression. Hide levels/scores.

Assessment Focus (For Implementers):
* Vocabulary Range: Check for basic terms (e.g., "good," "talk") vs. precise terms (e.g., "suits," "discuss"). Encourage event-specific language.
* Grammar and Syntax: Evaluate sentence structure (e.g., simple present vs. complex structures). Suggest clarity improvements.
* Connection to Phase 1: Ensure responses reference the cultural theme or teamwork from Phase 1.
* Coherence and Negotiation: Assess clarity in proposals, politeness in negotiation, and cohesion in confirmations. Deliver feedback as team encouragement.

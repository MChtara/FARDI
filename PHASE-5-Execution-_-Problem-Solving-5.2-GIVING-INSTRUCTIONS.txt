PHASE 5: Execution & Problem-Solving
Sub-phase 5.2: Giving Instructions to Volunteers
PHASE OVERVIEW
• Phase Title: Phase 5: Execution & Problem-Solving - 5.2 Giving Instructions to Volunteers
• Description: In this sub-phase, students practice writing clear, polite, and structured instructions for volunteers during the Global Cultures Festival (e.g., welcoming guests, guiding to booths, managing queues, handling small emergencies). Focus is on imperative form, sequencing words (first, then, after that), polite language (please, thank you, could you), safety reminders, and clarity for non-native speakers. The phase follows the 5E model to build autonomous writing of instructional texts (bullet lists, short paragraphs, spoken-style messages).
• Source Files: volunteer_instruction_template.doc, sample_task_card.pdf, polite_imperative_guide.pdf
• Scoring Main: A2=1pt, B1=2pts, B2=3pts, C1=4pts. Total = 12 to proceed.
• Scoring Remedial: 6/8 correct to pass.
STEP 1: Engage
• Description: Reconnect students with the festival context and the role of volunteers. Ms. Mabrouki shows a short video clip of volunteers helping at a real cultural event (e.g., festival ushering scene: https://youtube.com/shorts/lg7adyHPC7U?si=90bXTr0QJnJDHRnK). She asks: "What instructions would you give volunteers? How can we make them clear and polite?" To activate vocabulary, students play "Wordshake" individually for 3 minutes to form words related to giving instructions (target: please, thank you, first, then, after, careful, help, guide, welcome, queue, safety, listen). This prepares them to discuss real-life volunteer tasks.
• Learning Outcomes (A2/B1/B2/C1):
A2: Can name basic instruction words (e.g., "please", "help").
B1: Can describe simple volunteer tasks (e.g., "Guide people to booth").
B2: Can explain why instructions must be clear/polite (e.g., "Say please because polite").
C1: Can analyze effective instruction-giving (e.g., "Sequencing words help understanding; polite tone builds cooperation").

STEP 3: Explain
Sub-phase 5.2: Giving Instructions to Volunteers - Phase 5: Execution & Problem-Solving
• Description: Formalize concepts by defining and explaining the key elements of effective instructions for volunteers during the Global Cultures Festival. Incorporate glossary terms: "please", "thank you", "first", "then", "next", "after that", "finally", "careful", "safety", "guide", "welcome", "help", "queue", "clear", "polite". Students watch short explainer videos and real volunteer briefing examples, then practice definitions and explanations through flashcard matching, spelling games, and structured speaking/writing tasks.

To gamify, begin with "Wordshake" (link: https://learnenglishteens.britishcouncil.org/vocabulary/vocabulary-games/wordshake) - 3 minutes to form words like "please", "thank you", "first", "then", "careful", "guide", "welcome", "safety", then explain one term.
• Learning Outcomes (A2/B1/B2/C1):
A2: Can define basic instruction words (e.g., "Please is polite word").
B1: Can explain simple functions with examples (e.g., "First is step one because order").
B2: Can describe why sequencing and politeness matter (e.g., "Say please because volunteers feel respected").
C1: Can analyze integrated instruction-giving (e.g., "Polite openers + logical sequencing + safety reminders foster cooperation and reduce errors").
